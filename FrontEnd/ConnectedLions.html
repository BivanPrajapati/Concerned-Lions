Roger Federer

Roger Federer (born August 8, 1981, Basel, Switzerland) is a Swiss tennis player who dominated the sport in the early 21st century with his exceptional all-around game. He won a record eight Wimbledon titles, and in 2018 he became the first player to claim 20 Grand Slam men’s singles titles.

Career history

Roger Federer’s career was a masterclass in elegance, longevity, and dominance. Bursting onto the scene with his first Grand Slam win at Wimbledon in 2003, he went on to win 20 major titles, spend a record 310 weeks as World No. 1, and redefine the sport with his fluid style, precision, and grace. After an era of unmatched dominance in the mid-2000s, he adapted his game to face new rivals like Nadal and Djokovic, enjoying a remarkable resurgence in his mid-30s. Even through injury setbacks and a late-career decline, Federer remained a beloved icon and global ambassador for tennis. He retired in 2022, leaving behind a legacy as one of the greatest and most admired athletes in sports history.

Prior To Professional Debut

Started Playing tennis from a young age. In 1995, at age 13, Federer won the U14 Swiss national junior championship and was subsequently invited to train at the prestigious National Tennis Centre in the French-speaking Écublens, where he enrolled shortly after his 14th birthday

Early Career

  • Turned pro in 1998 at the age of 17.
  • Showed promise early by winning the Wimbledon boys' singles and doubles titles in 1998.
  • In 1999, he made his first Grand Slam appearance and cracked the top 100 in the ATP rankings.
  • By 2006 he had6 Grand Slam titles and held the World No. 1 ranking for nearly 2 years

2006 Season

  • Federer's 2006 season is widely regarded as one of the greatest seasons in tennis history
  • Win–Loss Record: 92–5
  • Titles: 12 titles (including 3 Grand Slams, 4 Masters 1000s, and the ATP Finals)
  • Federer was at peak dominance, showcasing a blend of elegance, power, and tactical brilliance.

2007 - 2011 Season

  • From 2007 to 2009, Federer remained the standard-bearer of excellence
  • 2010 to 2011 period saw Federer transition from undisputed king to a founding member of the "Big Three"
  • This period was a transition from Federer’s dominance to a Big Three era

2012 Season

  • Reclaimed World No. 1
  • Dominated the tour during the first half of the year, but slowed down in the latter half.
  • Maintained high-level consistency, showing few signs of age at 30–31.
  • Federer proved that his "prime" wasn’t over just yet.

Post 2012

  • Grand Slam Titles: 3 (2017 AO, 2017 Wimbledon, 2018 AO)
  • Reinvented his game to stay competitive into his mid-to-late 30s
  • Remained the sport’s classiest and most influential ambassador
